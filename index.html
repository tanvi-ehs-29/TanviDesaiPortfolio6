Hi, I'm Tanvi Desai

My career goal is to become a software Engineer who can create technology which helps those living with Parkinson's disease. Someday and soon, I hope to make digital tools that can help detect the disease early so that they can get the best treatment and cure themselves sooner. I’m planning to achieve this goal by talking with professional therapists, joining research projects in both the computer science field and a little more about people's health, and expanding my nonprofit which focuses mainly on Parkinson's support. I am already speaking with a professional therapist to learn more about the disease and understand how I can better help people who are affected by it. This goal is very important to me because most people in my family are praised for becoming doctors, and my entire adolescence was about helping others, and I want to continue that same idea in my own way, a different way. Instead of following their pathway of going into the medical field, I want to use artificial intelligence to make a difference and help improve early disease detection.

Throughout middle school and the beginning of high school, I’ve built strong skills including, problem solving, communication, and the ability to stay focused on hard tasks. Working on projects for my nonprofit has taught me how to deal with planning events, working with adults, and communicating in a professional way. These experiences show that I can stay organized, think wisely, and work well with others by communicating.

Some of my most meaningful experiences or achievements include enlarging my nonprofit, looking at research opportunities as a high school freshman, and taking advanced classes to challenge myself. From these experiences, I have come to realize that I really enjoy helping others and finding answers and solutions that can make a real difference and impact. I also learned that I do well in group environments where teamwork, focus, and good communication matters. These skills help and connect to my long term goal of being able to use artificial intelligence to help detect Parkinson's disease early.

Thank you for taking the time to listen and learn about my goals and experiences. I hope you are motivated and interested in exploring the rest of my site to see more of my work, ideas, and future plans.
